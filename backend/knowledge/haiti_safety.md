# Haiti Safety Information

## Current Crisis Context

Haiti faces a multidimensional crisis driven by gang violence, political instability, economic collapse, and natural disaster vulnerability. Armed gangs control significant portions of Port-au-Prince and surrounding areas, creating a severe humanitarian emergency.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **Port-au-Prince**: Multiple neighborhoods under gang control (Cite Soleil, Martissant, Bel Air, Croix-des-Bouquets)
- **Artibonite Region**: Gang expansion into northern corridor, highway blockades
- **Gangs control 80%+ of Port-au-Prince**: Including main roads and port access
- **Southern Peninsula**: Still recovering from 2021 earthquake, gang influence spreading

**Relatively Safer Areas:**
- Parts of northern Haiti (Cap-Haitien area)
- Some rural areas away from main highways
- Jacmel and parts of southeast department
- Note: Safety is relative - no area is fully secure

## Health and Medical Access

**Hospitals:**
- **Port-au-Prince**: Hospital de l'Universite d'Etat d'Haiti (HUEH), MSF Tabarre Trauma Center, MSF Drouillard Emergency Center, Bernard Mevs Hospital
- **Cap-Haitien**: Hopital Universitaire Justinien
- **Les Cayes**: Hospital Immaculee Conception
- **Jeremie**: Hopital Saint-Antoine

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Gunshot and violence-related trauma (primary health burden)
- Cholera resurgence since October 2022
- Malaria and dengue increasing
- Severe malnutrition among children
- Maternal mortality very high
- Many hospitals inaccessible due to gang-controlled areas
- Medical supply shortages critical

**Healthcare Access:**
- Many hospitals have been attacked, looted, or forced to close
- MSF operates critical trauma care but overwhelmed
- Healthcare workers fleeing the country (brain drain)
- Medications scarce and expensive

## Water and Food Security

**Water:**
- Municipal water systems barely functional in Port-au-Prince
- Water trucking essential in many areas
- UNICEF and NGOs support water distribution
- Cholera contamination risk - always treat water
- Bottled water available but expensive

**Food:**
- WFP distributes food to millions
- Food prices extremely high due to supply disruptions
- Gang blockades of main roads interrupt food supply chains
- Hunger crisis affecting over 5 million people (nearly half the population)
- Acute malnutrition in children rising rapidly

## Displacement

**Internal Displacement:**
- Over 700,000 IDPs (primarily from gang violence in Port-au-Prince)
- Displacement sites in schools, churches, public buildings
- Many IDPs in host communities
- Conditions in displacement sites dire - overcrowding, GBV, disease
- Gang incursions into some displacement sites

**Movement Restrictions:**
- Gangs control main highways including Route Nationale 1 (north) and Route Nationale 2 (south)
- Travel between Port-au-Prince and other cities extremely dangerous
- Kidnapping for ransom widespread on major roads
- Air transport may be only safe option for some routes

## Security

**Gang Violence:**
- Armed gangs control approximately 80% of Port-au-Prince
- Widespread kidnapping for ransom
- Sexual violence used as weapon by gangs
- Sniper fire in contested areas
- Arson and mass displacement used as tactics

**Safety Recommendations:**
- Avoid traveling through gang-controlled areas
- Do not display phones, jewelry, or valuables
- Avoid travel after dark
- Keep vehicle windows up and doors locked
- Travel in groups when possible
- Know alternate routes for every journey

**Kidnapping:**
- Extremely high kidnapping rate
- Targets: all demographics including Haitians and foreigners
- Ransom demands range from hundreds to millions of dollars
- Report immediately to PNH and ICRC

## Specific Risks

**Sexual and Gender-Based Violence:**
- Systematic use of rape by gangs as tool of control
- UNFPA and local organizations provide some services
- Many survivors unable to access medical care due to insecurity
- Emergency contraception and PEP (post-exposure prophylaxis) critical within 72 hours

**Child Protection:**
- Gang recruitment of children
- Child exploitation and trafficking
- Schools closed in gang-controlled areas
- UNICEF protection programs where accessible
- Orphanage exploitation and irregular adoption concerns

## Communication

**Phone and Internet:**
- Mobile networks: Digicel (primary), Natcom
- Mobile money (MonCash via Digicel) primary payment method
- Internet available but slow and expensive
- Power grid extremely unreliable - solar and generator dependent
- Cell towers sometimes targeted/damaged by gangs

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- Haitian Gourde (HTG) and US dollars both circulate
- MonCash (mobile money) widely used for payments and transfers
- Banking limited to major cities, ATMs unreliable
- Western Union and money transfer services important
- Cash essential for daily transactions
- Economy largely informal

Remember: Haiti's crisis is severe and worsening. Gang control of territory makes movement extremely dangerous. Humanitarian access is severely constrained. Always verify current conditions through local contacts and humanitarian organizations. Travel only when absolutely necessary and with proper security assessment.
