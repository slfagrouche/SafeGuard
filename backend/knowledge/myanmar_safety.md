# Myanmar Safety Information

## Current Conflict Context

Since the military coup of February 2021, Myanmar has been engulfed in widespread armed conflict between the military junta (Tatmadaw/SAC) and a combination of People's Defence Forces (PDF), ethnic armed organizations (EAOs), and the National Unity Government (NUG). The conflict has intensified dramatically, with resistance forces making significant territorial gains.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **Sagaing Region**: Heaviest fighting, widespread airstrikes and village burning
- **Magway Region**: Active conflict, displacement
- **Chin State**: Significant resistance activity, severe destruction
- **Kayah (Karenni) State**: Heavy fighting, Loikaw largely destroyed
- **Shan State**: Multiple EAOs and PDF fighting, complex dynamics
- **Rakhine State**: Arakan Army operations, Rohingya population at risk
- **Karen (Kayin) State**: KNU/KNLA and PDF operations against military

**Relatively Safer Areas:**
- Parts of Yangon (limited strikes, urban tension)
- Mandalay city center (tense but lower armed conflict)
- Some areas controlled by established EAOs with stable governance

## Health and Medical Access

**Hospitals:**
- **Yangon**: Yangon General Hospital, North Okkalapa Hospital, Insein General Hospital
- **Mandalay**: Mandalay General Hospital
- **Conflict Areas**: Military has targeted healthcare facilities, many destroyed
- Ethnic health organizations operate health systems in EAO areas
- Community health workers provide primary care in resistance areas

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- COVID-19 ongoing with collapsed healthcare system
- Malaria endemic in border and jungle areas
- Dengue fever seasonal outbreaks
- TB and HIV treatment disrupted
- Maternal mortality rising due to collapsed health system
- Mental health crisis - trauma, PTSD widespread

**Healthcare Crisis:**
- Post-coup Civil Disobedience Movement (CDM) saw many healthcare workers leave government system
- Military-run hospitals often avoided by civilians (fear of arrest)
- Private clinics and ethnic health organizations filling gaps
- Severe medication shortages

## Water and Food Security

**Water:**
- Urban water systems generally functional
- Rural areas dependent on wells and rain collection
- Conflict areas face water infrastructure damage
- Dry season water scarcity in central dry zone

**Food:**
- WFP access severely restricted by military
- Agricultural production disrupted by conflict
- Prices rising due to currency collapse and supply chain disruption
- Rural areas face food insecurity due to displacement and crop destruction

## Displacement

**Internal Displacement:**
- Over 2.7 million IDPs (massive increase post-coup)
- Many displaced into jungle and forested areas without shelter
- Cross-border displacement to Thailand, India, Bangladesh
- Rohingya crisis continues - camps in Cox's Bazar, Bangladesh

**Rohingya Situation:**
- Over 600,000 Rohingya remain in Rakhine State with severe movement restrictions
- Camps in central Rakhine with limited services
- Subject to violence from both military and some EAOs
- No clear path to citizenship or freedom of movement

## Security

**Military Tactics:**
- Airstrikes on civilian areas (using fighter jets and attack helicopters)
- Village burning and forced displacement
- Arbitrary detention and torture
- Internet shutdowns in conflict areas
- Use of human shields and landmines

**Landmines:**
- Massive increase in landmine use by all parties since coup
- One of highest new landmine casualty rates globally
- Stay on paved roads and well-known paths
- Never enter recently abandoned military positions

**Checkpoints:**
- Military checkpoints in urban and semi-urban areas
- Carry national ID (CSC - Citizenship Scrutiny Card) at all times
- Phone checks for content at checkpoints common
- Avoid carrying politically sensitive material on devices

**Communication Security:**
- Internet monitored by military
- VPN use technically illegal but widely practiced
- Phone searches at checkpoints
- Encrypted messaging apps (Signal) recommended but risky if found
- SIM card registration mandatory

## Specific Risks

**Arbitrary Detention:**
- Widespread arrests of perceived opposition supporters
- Journalists, activists, healthcare workers particularly at risk
- Torture and deaths in detention reported
- ICRC has limited detention access

**Gender-Based Violence:**
- Military documented using sexual violence as weapon
- Limited services available in conflict areas
- Community-based support in some resistance areas

**Ethnic Persecution:**
- Rohingya face severe discrimination and violence
- Ethnic minorities in conflict areas face collective punishment
- Military targets civilian populations in ethnic areas

## Communication

**Phone and Internet:**
- Mobile networks: MPT, Mytel (military-linked), Ooredoo, Telenor (sold to local consortium)
- Internet throttling and shutdowns frequent
- Mobile banking (Wave Money, KBZPay) widely used
- Power outages common - solar charging essential in rural areas

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- Myanmar Kyat severely devalued since coup
- US dollars and Thai Baht valuable for larger transactions
- Mobile banking (Wave Money, KBZPay) widely used
- ATMs functional in major cities but withdrawal limits
- Cash essential - banking system stressed
- Informal money transfer systems active

Remember: Myanmar is in a state of civil war with rapidly changing frontlines. Information can become outdated within days. The military restricts humanitarian access and information flow. Always verify current conditions through local contacts and trusted information sources.
