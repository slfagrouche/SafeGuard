# Ukraine Emergency Contacts and Resources

## Humanitarian Context

Since February 2022, Ukraine has experienced large-scale armed conflict affecting much of the country. The humanitarian situation varies significantly by region, with active combat zones in the east and south, periodic infrastructure attacks nationwide, and millions displaced internally and abroad.

**Current Situation:**
- Active frontlines primarily in eastern Ukraine (Donetsk, Luhansk oblasts)
- Ongoing attacks on energy infrastructure causing power outages nationwide
- Millions internally displaced (IDPs)
- Western Ukraine relatively stable but not immune to strikes
- Air raid alerts routine across the country

**Most Affected Areas:**
- Donetsk and Luhansk oblasts (active combat)
- Kherson, Zaporizhzhia oblasts (frontline areas and occupied zones)
- Kharkiv oblast (periodic shelling and strikes)
- Mykolaiv, Dnipropetrovsk oblasts (occasional strikes)

## Key Organizations

**Ukrainian Red Cross Society:**
- Primary humanitarian organization providing nationwide assistance
- Emergency hotline: +38-044-238-5035
- Services: First aid, psychosocial support, humanitarian aid distribution, tracing services
- Active in most regions including frontline areas
- Operates humanitarian aid distribution centers and shelter programs

**United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) Ukraine:**
- Protection and assistance for displaced persons
- Hotline: 0-800-507-309 (toll-free within Ukraine)
- Registration of IDPs, legal assistance, humanitarian aid
- Operates "Protection Hotline" for urgent protection concerns
- Cash assistance programs for displaced families

**World Health Organization (WHO) Ukraine:**
- Health sector coordination during emergencies
- Support to Ukrainian healthcare system
- Emergency medical supplies
- Coordinates with Ministry of Health
- Contact through local health facilities or Ukrainian Red Cross

**International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC):**
- Protection of civilians and prisoners
- Family reunification and tracing
- Kyiv office: +38-044-461-8590
- Visits places of detention
- Supports healthcare facilities near frontlines

**UN Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA):**
- Coordinates overall humanitarian response
- Information sharing on humanitarian situation
- Works with Ukrainian authorities and aid organizations

- **102** - Police

**Additional Resources:**
- Ukrainian Red Cross Hotline: +38-044-238-5035

**Regional Emergency Services:**
- Each oblast has regional emergency service administration

## Air Raid Safety

**Air Raid Alert Protocol:**
- Download Air Raid Alert app (Повітряна тривога) for smartphone notifications
- When siren sounds or alert received: proceed to nearest shelter immediately
- Typical warning time: 5-15 minutes for missile attacks, less for drones
- Stay in shelter until "all clear" signal

**Shelter Locations:**
- Kyiv: Metro stations are primary shelters
- Other cities: Designated basement shelters marked with blue/yellow signs
- Buildings: Interior corridors, basements, rooms without windows
- If caught outside: Seek nearest building entrance, basement, or lie flat in ditch

**Shelter Supplies (keep ready):**
- Important documents in waterproof folder
- Phone with charged power bank
- Water and snacks
- Basic first aid kit
- Flashlight
- Warm clothing/blanket

## Evacuation Corridors

**Government Evacuation Program:**
- Ministry of Reintegration coordinates evacuations from frontline areas
- Hotline: 1548 (information on evacuation)
- Free evacuation provided from dangerous areas
- Designated assembly points in each community

**Evacuation Train Services:**
- Ukrzaliznytsia (Ukrainian Railways) operates evacuation trains
- Often free or heavily subsidized from frontline areas
- Pet-friendly evacuation options available
- Booking through railway website or at stations

**Road Evacuation:**
- Humanitarian corridors announced for specific areas during active combat
- Follow National Police and military guidance
- Travel during daylight when possible
- Roads may have checkpoints - carry ID

## IDP Support and Registration

**IDP Registration:**
- Register at local social protection office (USZN) in your current location
- Brings eligibility for financial assistance and social services
- Required documents: passport/ID, proof of displacement
- Registration can be done online: https://www.mil.gov.ua/

**IDP Assistance Programs:**
- Monthly cash assistance (amount varies)
- Access to healthcare in new location
- Educational support for children
- Housing assistance programs (limited)
- Employment support services

**IDP Accommodation:**
- Collective centers in many cities (temporary housing)
- Host family programs coordinated by NGOs
- Rent support programs (limited availability)
- Priority access to dormitories and social housing

## Heating Centers (Winter)

**"Points of Invincibility" (Punky Nezlamnosti):**
- Established nationwide in response to infrastructure attacks
- Provide: heating, electricity for charging, internet, hot beverages, basic medical care
- Operating locations announced by local authorities during power outages
- Free to use, no registration required
- Ask local authorities for nearest location or check municipal websites/Telegram channels

## Financial and Practical Support

**Banking:**
- ATMs functional in most areas (when electricity available)
- Digital banking widely used (Monobank, PrivatBank apps)
- Cash recommended as backup during power outages
- International transfers: SWIFT system disrupted for some banks, use alternative services

**Humanitarian Aid Distribution:**
- Ukrainian Red Cross distribution centers nationwide
- Local charity organizations (check municipal social services)
- Religious organizations providing assistance
- Registration often required, bring ID documents

**Employment Support:**
- IDP employment programs through employment centers
- Remote work opportunities expanding
- Vocational training programs available
- EU temporary protection allows work in EU countries

## Communication

**Internet and Mobile:**
- Major operators: Kyivstar, Vodafone Ukraine, lifecell
- Generally functional except in occupied/combat zones
- Power outages affect coverage - may be intermittent
- Starlink available in many areas for backup connectivity

**Information Sources:**
- Air Alert app for air raid warnings (essential)
- Telegram channels of local authorities for current information
- Ukrainian Emergency Service official channels
- Trusted news sources for national situation

**Document Safety:**
- Digital copies of all important documents
- Store on phone and secure cloud storage
- Physical copies in waterproof case
- Birth certificates, passports, education documents, property documents

## Security Considerations

**Checkpoints:**
- Ukrainian military checkpoints throughout country, especially near frontlines
- Always carry valid ID (passport or Ukrainian ID card)
- Follow instructions from military/police
- Curfews in effect in many areas - know local curfew hours

**Unexploded Ordnance (UXO):**
- Widespread UXO contamination in liberated and frontline areas
- Never touch unknown objects
- Stay on paved roads in affected areas
- SESU (State Emergency Service) demining teams clearing contaminated areas
- Report UXO: 101 or 102

**Occupied Territories:**
- Humanitarian situation severe in Russian-occupied areas
- Limited access for international organizations
- High risk of human rights violations
- Evacuation opportunities limited and dangerous

## Mental Health and Psychosocial Support

**Psychological Support:**
- National hotline: 0-800-500-225 (24/7, free, anonymous)
- Ukrainian Red Cross psychosocial support programs
- Online therapy platforms expanding
- Community support groups for IDPs and affected populations
- Child-specific trauma support through schools and NGOs

**Support for Veterans and Military:**
- Veterans' hotline: 0-800-304-577
- Specialized PTSD treatment centers
- Rehabilitation programs for wounded
- Family support programs

Remember: Ukraine is a large country with regional variations in security situation. Always follow guidance from local authorities, monitor air raid alerts, and register as IDP if displaced to access services and support. Keep essential documents and emergency supplies ready.
