# Sudan Safety Information

## Current Conflict Context

Sudan has been experiencing severe armed conflict since April 2023, primarily between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF). The conflict has resulted in widespread civilian casualties, mass displacement, and humanitarian crisis affecting millions.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **Khartoum**: Heavy urban combat, divided control, infrastructure collapse
- **Darfur Region**: Particularly West Darfur (El Geneina), ethnic violence and displacement
- **El Fasher (North Darfur)**: Ongoing siege and humanitarian access challenges
- **Kordofan**: South Kordofan and West Kordofan experiencing conflict spillover

**Relatively Safer Areas (as of latest reports):**
- Northern State and parts of River Nile State
- Eastern Sudan (Port Sudan now serving as de facto capital)
- Some areas of Gezira State (situation fluid)

## Key Humanitarian Organizations

**International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC):**
- Khartoum Office: +249-183-784-490
- Provides medical support, surgical care, family reunification
- Operates mobile health clinics in accessible areas

**Médecins Sans Frontières (Doctors Without Borders):**
- Emergency medical care in conflict-affected areas
- Mobile clinics in Darfur and Khartoum
- No specific hotline - seek them at medical facilities

**United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR):**
- Coordinates refugee and IDP assistance
- Protection services for displaced populations
- Port Sudan Office: +249-311-828-xxx (check current number)

**World Food Programme (WFP):**
- Food distribution in accessible areas
- Works through local partners in many locations
- Emergency food assistance for IDPs

**Sudan Red Crescent Society (SRCS):**
- Local emergency response and ambulance services
- Headquarters: +249-183-784-490
- Community-based support and first aid

## Safe Corridors and IDP Camps

**Evacuation Routes (as of latest information):**
- Khartoum to Port Sudan (Red Sea State) - hazardous, requires coordination
- Khartoum to Northern State - variable safety depending on control areas
- Cross-border evacuations to Egypt, Chad, South Sudan, Central African Republic

**Major IDP Camp Locations:**
- **Kassala State**: Multiple IDP camps, humanitarian access generally maintained
- **Gedaref State**: IDP settlements with WFP/UNHCR presence
- **Northern State**: Smaller IDP concentrations, more stable
- **Port Sudan and Red Sea State**: Large IDP populations, services stretched
- **Darfur Region**: Multiple camp locations, security situation variable

**Camp Safety Considerations:**
- Register with UNHCR or local authorities upon arrival
- Camps may be overcrowded with limited resources
- Women and children should not travel alone to water/sanitation points
- Report security incidents to camp management immediately

## Water and Food Distribution Points

**Water Access:**
- Many urban water systems non-functional due to electricity cuts
- Seek water distribution points organized by WFP, UNICEF, or local NGOs
- In IDP camps, designated water collection points with scheduled hours
- Treat water when possible (boil 1 minute or use purification tablets)

**Food Distribution:**
- WFP coordinates food assistance in many accessible areas
- Distribution typically occurs at designated points on scheduled days
- Bring registration documentation and family identification
- Vulnerable groups (children, elderly, pregnant women) may receive priority

**Local Markets:**
- Where functional, markets may have limited food supplies at inflated prices
- Cash may not be widely accepted - barter systems emerging in some areas
- Security at markets can be unpredictable - travel in groups if possible

## Health and Medical Access

**Functioning Hospitals (status variable):**
- **Khartoum**: Most hospitals non-functional or operating at minimal capacity
  - Bashair Teaching Hospital (limited services)
  - Royal Care Hospital (if operational)
- **Port Sudan**: Turkish Hospital, Port Sudan Teaching Hospital
- **Kassala**: Kassala Teaching Hospital
- **El Obeid**: El Obeid Teaching Hospital (if accessible)

**ICRC Surgical Facilities:**
- ICRC supports several hospitals with surgical capacity for war-wounded
- Contact ICRC directly for current facility locations

**Medical Supply Shortages:**
- Critical shortage of medications, surgical supplies, blood products
- Chronic disease medications (diabetes, hypertension) extremely scarce
- Bring essential medications if evacuating - resupply may be impossible

## Security and Movement

**Checkpoints:**
- Multiple armed groups control different areas with numerous checkpoints
- Carry identification documents at all times
- Avoid travel at night
- Do not photograph military installations or checkpoints
- Follow instructions from armed personnel - do not argue

**Communication:**
- Internet and cellular service disrupted in many areas
- Electricity cuts affect phone charging - power banks essential
- Satellite phones highly valued - use discreetly
- Some international SIM cards work better than local in certain areas

**Documentation:**
- Keep copies of important documents separate from originals
- Passport, national ID, medical records, vaccination cards
- Digital photos of documents stored on phone/USB drive

## Specific Risks

**Gender-Based Violence:**
- Reports of widespread sexual violence in conflict-affected areas
- Women and girls should not travel alone if possible
- Seek safe houses or women's protection spaces in camps
- Report incidents to humanitarian protection officers

**Child Protection:**
- Risk of child recruitment by armed forces/groups
- Keep children close in displacement settings
- Register children with UNHCR for protection services
- Document any family separation immediately

**Ethnic Targeting:**
- Conflict has ethnic dimensions, particularly in Darfur
- Certain ethnic groups face targeted violence
- Seek protection from UNHCR or other international organizations
- Avoid discussing ethnicity at checkpoints if possible

## Financial Considerations

**Banking:**
- ATMs largely non-functional in conflict areas
- Mobile money services disrupted
- Cash is critical - keep small denominations
- US dollars may be preferred in some transactions
- Hide cash in multiple locations on your person

**Inflation:**
- Severe inflation has made basic goods expensive
- Prices can vary dramatically between locations
- Black market exchange rates diverge from official rates

Remember: The situation in Sudan is highly dynamic and changes rapidly. Information provided here may become outdated. Always seek the most current information from humanitarian organizations on the ground and follow their guidance.
