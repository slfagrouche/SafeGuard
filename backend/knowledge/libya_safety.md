# Libya Safety Information

## Current Context

Libya has been in various states of conflict since the 2011 revolution. The country remains divided between rival administrations in the east and west, with numerous armed groups, tribal militias, and foreign fighters operating across the territory. Humanitarian needs persist, particularly for displaced populations and migrants/refugees.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **Southern Libya**: Tribal conflicts, migration routes, limited governance (Fezzan region)
- **Sirte and central corridor**: Contested territory between rival factions
- **Benghazi and eastern cities**: Post-conflict reconstruction, armed group presence
- **Western coastal areas**: Periodic armed group clashes

**Relatively Safer Areas:**
- Parts of Tripoli (when no fighting)
- Parts of Misrata
- Some western coastal towns

## Health and Medical Access

**Hospitals:**
- **Tripoli**: Tripoli Central Hospital, Mitiga Hospital, Abu Salim Hospital, Tripoli Medical Centre
- **Benghazi**: Benghazi Medical Centre, Al-Jala Hospital
- **Misrata**: Misrata Central Hospital
- **Sabha**: Sabha Medical Centre

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Healthcare system degraded since 2011
- Many hospitals damaged or understaffed
- Medication shortages for chronic diseases
- Mental health services extremely limited
- COVID-19 ongoing with low vaccination rates
- Migration health issues (TB, malaria among migrants)

## Displacement and Migration

**Internal Displacement:**
- Hundreds of thousands of IDPs across the country
- Tawergha community displacement since 2011
- Southern Libya displacement from tribal conflicts

**Migration Crisis:**
- Libya is major transit country for migration to Europe
- Migrants and refugees face arbitrary detention, exploitation, trafficking
- Detention centers with documented human rights abuses
- UNHCR and IOM provide limited protection and evacuation
- Sea rescue operations for Mediterranean crossings

## Security

**Armed Groups:**
- Numerous militias and armed groups aligned with rival governments
- Tribal militias in southern Libya
- Foreign fighters and mercenaries present
- ISIS remnants in desert areas

**General Safety:**
- Carry identification at all times
- Avoid military installations and armed group headquarters
- Do not travel at night
- Kidnapping risk for foreigners and Libyans alike
- Roads unsafe due to armed checkpoints and carjacking

**Landmines and UXO:**
- Contamination in former conflict areas
- Sirte, Benghazi southern suburbs, Tripoli outskirts
- UNMAS conducts clearance operations

## Communication

**Phone and Internet:**
- Mobile networks: Libyana, Al-Madar
- Internet available in urban areas
- Social media widely used for information
- Power outages frequent

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- Libyan Dinar (LYD)
- Cash economy dominant
- ATMs available in major cities but often non-functional
- US dollars and euros useful for some transactions
- Money transfer services available

Remember: Libya's political and security situation is fragmented. Different areas are controlled by different armed groups, and the situation can change rapidly. Always verify current conditions through local contacts and humanitarian organizations.
