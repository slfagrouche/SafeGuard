# Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) Safety Information

## Current Conflict Context

The DRC, particularly eastern provinces, faces one of the world's most protracted humanitarian crises. Multiple armed groups, including M23, ADF (Allied Democratic Forces), and numerous local militias, operate across North Kivu, South Kivu, and Ituri provinces. Over 7 million people are internally displaced.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **North Kivu**: M23 offensive around Goma, Masisi, Rutshuru; ADF attacks in Beni territory
- **South Kivu**: Armed group activity in Fizi, Uvira, Kalehe territories
- **Ituri**: CODECO and ADF attacks, ethnic violence in Djugu and Irumu
- **Tanganyika**: Intercommunal violence, displacement
- **Kasai region**: Periodic militia resurgence

**Relatively Safer Areas:**
- Kinshasa (capital, western DRC)
- Lubumbashi and southern Katanga
- Parts of Equateur province
- Matadi and Bas-Congo region

## Health and Medical Access

**Hospitals:**
- **Goma**: HEAL Africa Hospital, Charité Maternité Hospital, ICRC-supported Ndosho Hospital
- **Bukavu**: Panzi Hospital (Dr. Mukwege's renowned facility for GBV survivors), Bukavu General Hospital
- **Kinshasa**: Centre Hospitalier de Kinshasa (CHK), Cliniques Universitaires de Kinshasa
- **Bunia**: Bunia General Hospital, MSF-supported facilities
- **Beni**: Beni General Hospital

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Ebola outbreaks recurring (most recently in eastern provinces)
- Cholera endemic in eastern DRC, particularly around Lake Kivu
- Measles outbreaks among displaced populations
- Malaria: Hyperendemic across the country (leading cause of death)
- Mpox (monkeypox) outbreaks
- Severe malnutrition in conflict areas
- Limited healthcare infrastructure in rural and conflict areas

**Disease Prevention:**
- Malaria: Sleep under treated mosquito nets, use repellent
- Cholera: Treat all water, wash hands frequently
- Ebola: Avoid contact with sick individuals, report symptoms immediately
- Seek vaccination where campaigns are active

## Water and Food Security

**Water:**
- Safe water access extremely limited in conflict zones
- UNICEF and NGOs support water infrastructure in camps
- Lake water NOT safe without treatment (cholera risk)
- Boil or treat all water before drinking

**Food:**
- WFP distributes emergency food in accessible areas
- Markets functional in urban areas but expensive
- Agricultural production disrupted by conflict in eastern DRC
- Severe food insecurity affecting over 25 million people

## Displacement

**IDP Camps (Eastern DRC):**
- Multiple sites around Goma (Bulengo, Lushagala, others)
- Over 2 million IDPs in North Kivu alone
- Camp conditions often dire - overcrowding, limited services
- Periodic armed group incursions into camp areas

**Movement:**
- Travel by road extremely dangerous in eastern DRC
- Ambushes and armed group checkpoints common
- Main highways (RN2, RN4) periodically unsafe
- MONUSCO convoys sometimes available for humanitarian movement
- River transport used but also subject to armed group interference

## Security

**Armed Groups:**
- M23: Controls significant territory in North Kivu near Rwanda border
- ADF: Operates in Beni-Lubero area, linked to ISIS
- CODECO: Active in Ituri, ethnic militia
- Mai-Mai groups: Numerous local militias across eastern DRC
- FDLR: Rwandan Hutu militia operating in eastern DRC

**Checkpoints:**
- Government (FARDC) and armed group checkpoints widespread
- Carry identification at all times
- Avoid displaying valuables or electronics
- Informal taxation/extortion common at checkpoints
- MONUSCO peacekeepers present at some locations

**Landmines:**
- Some contamination in eastern DRC from previous conflicts
- Stay on well-traveled paths
- Report suspicious objects to UNMAS or MONUSCO

## Specific Risks

**Sexual and Gender-Based Violence:**
- DRC has extremely high rates of conflict-related sexual violence
- Panzi Hospital in Bukavu provides specialized care for survivors
- UNHCR and UNFPA protection services available
- Safe houses and women's protection spaces in some areas

**Child Protection:**
- High risk of child recruitment by armed groups
- Child labor in mining operations (cobalt, coltan)
- UNICEF child protection programs in accessible areas
- Separated children should be reported to ICRC for tracing

**Ethnic Targeting:**
- Intercommunal violence along ethnic lines in Ituri and other areas
- Avoid discussing ethnicity at checkpoints
- Seek protection from UNHCR or MONUSCO if targeted

## Communication

**Phone and Internet:**
- Mobile networks: Vodacom, Airtel, Orange DRC
- Coverage limited outside urban areas
- Mobile money (M-Pesa) widely used for transactions
- Internet available in major cities
- Power grid unreliable - solar charging common

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- Congolese Franc (CDF) is official currency
- US dollars widely accepted and preferred for larger transactions
- Mobile money (M-Pesa via Vodacom, Airtel Money) widely used
- Banking infrastructure limited outside Kinshasa, Lubumbashi, Goma
- Cash essential in rural and conflict areas

Remember: Eastern DRC is one of the world's most dangerous humanitarian environments. Armed group control areas shift frequently. Always seek current security information from MONUSCO, humanitarian organizations, and local contacts before any movement.
