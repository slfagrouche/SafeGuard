# Afghanistan Safety Information

## Current Context

Since August 2021, Afghanistan has been under Taliban (Islamic Emirate) control. The humanitarian crisis has deepened with economic collapse, severe restrictions on women and girls, and ongoing security threats from ISIS-K (Islamic State Khorasan Province).

**Key Challenges:**
- Economic collapse following international sanctions and aid reduction
- Severe restrictions on women's education and employment
- ISIS-K attacks targeting civilians and Taliban forces
- Natural disasters (earthquakes, floods) compounding crisis
- Over 28 million people in need of humanitarian assistance

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **Kabul**: Economic hardship, security threats, ISIS-K attacks
- **Nangarhar/Kunar**: ISIS-K stronghold areas, frequent attacks
- **Helmand/Kandahar**: Southern provinces, drug economy, periodic violence
- **Badakhshan/Takhar**: Northern earthquake-prone zones, poverty
- **Herat**: Western city, earthquake affected (October 2023), Iranian border tensions
- **Panjshir/Baghlan**: Resistance activity, security operations

**Relatively Safer Areas:**
- Parts of central Bamyan province
- Some areas of Balkh (Mazar-i-Sharif)
- Parts of western Herat (urban areas)

## Health and Medical Access

**Hospitals:**
- **Kabul**: Indira Gandhi Children's Hospital, Wazir Akbar Khan Hospital, French Medical Institute for Mothers and Children (FMIC), Ali Abad Hospital
- **Kandahar**: Mirwais Hospital (ICRC-supported), Kandahar Regional Hospital
- **Herat**: Herat Regional Hospital
- **Mazar-i-Sharif**: Balkh Regional Hospital
- **Kunduz**: Kunduz Regional Hospital (MSF previously supported)
- **Jalalabad**: Nangarhar Regional Hospital

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Measles outbreaks recurring
- Polio still endemic (one of last two countries)
- Acute watery diarrhea (AWD) outbreaks
- Severe malnutrition affecting millions of children
- Maternal mortality among highest in the world
- Mental health crisis (decades of conflict, PTSD widespread)

**Women's Healthcare:**
- Female healthcare workers severely restricted under Taliban rules
- Many women unable to access health services due to mahram (male guardian) requirement
- UNFPA and NGOs maintaining women's health programs where possible
- Maternal health services critical concern

## Water and Food Security

**Water:**
- Severe drought conditions in many provinces
- Municipal water systems non-functional in many areas
- UNICEF supports water trucking in drought-affected areas
- Wells drying up in many rural communities
- Treat all surface water before drinking

**Food:**
- WFP food distribution ongoing but funding shortfalls
- Markets functional but prices extremely high relative to purchasing power
- Currency (Afghani) has partially stabilized but purchasing power remains low
- Rural areas face severe food insecurity
- Poppy cultivation remains major economic driver in south

## Displacement

**Internal Displacement:**
- Over 6 million IDPs across the country
- Natural disaster displacement (earthquakes, floods) adding to conflict IDPs
- Many IDP settlements informal with minimal services
- Winter conditions extreme - hypothermia deaths common in camps

**Cross-Border Movement:**
- Pakistan border: Torkham and Chaman crossings - periodic closures, deportations of undocumented Afghans
- Iran border: Islam Qala crossing - large numbers of returnees, often involuntary
- Northern borders (Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan) largely closed

## Security

**ISIS-K Threats:**
- Suicide bombings and attacks primarily in Kabul, Nangarhar, Kunar
- Targets: mosques, schools, markets, government facilities
- Avoid large gatherings when possible
- Be aware of surroundings in crowded areas

**Taliban Checkpoints:**
- Extensive checkpoint network nationwide
- Carry valid identification (Tazkira national ID)
- Dress conservatively
- Women must be accompanied by mahram (male relative)
- Do not carry prohibited items (music, inappropriate media content per Taliban rules)

**Landmines and UXO:**
- Afghanistan among most mine-contaminated countries in the world
- Decades of conflict have left millions of devices
- Stay on paved roads and well-traveled paths
- HALO Trust and other organizations conducting clearance
- Report UXO through local authorities

## Restrictions on Women and Girls

**Current Restrictions:**
- Women barred from secondary and higher education
- Women barred from most employment outside health sector
- Mahram requirement for travel and accessing services
- Full face covering required in public
- Women's access to parks, gyms, public spaces restricted

**Available Support:**
- Underground education networks operating in some areas
- Women's health facilities maintained by humanitarian organizations
- UNFPA and UNICEF women's protection programs
- Some NGOs still employing women in health and humanitarian roles

## Communication

**Internet and Phone:**
- Mobile networks functional (Roshan, Etisalat, MTN, AWCC)
- Internet available in urban areas but monitored
- Social media platforms accessible but content monitored
- Power outages common - solar charging recommended
- VPN use may carry risk

## Financial Considerations

**Economy:**
- Afghan Afghani is the currency
- US dollars widely accepted, especially in Kabul
- Banking system partially functional but limited
- Hawala money transfer system widely used
- International banking sanctions limit transfers
- Cash economy dominant - keep small denominations

Remember: Afghanistan's situation is complex and evolving. Taliban policies change with limited notice. Humanitarian access varies by province and is often negotiated. Always verify current conditions through humanitarian organizations operating in your specific area.
