# Sahel Region Safety Information (Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger)

## Current Conflict Context

The Sahel region faces a severe and worsening security crisis involving armed extremist groups (JNIM/AQIM affiliates, Islamic State in the Greater Sahara/ISGS), military juntas, and intercommunal violence. Mali, Burkina Faso, and Niger have all experienced military coups and the withdrawal of international forces, leading to security vacuums.

**Mali - Most Affected Areas:**
- **Northern Mali**: Kidal, Timbuktu, Gao regions - armed group control and clashes
- **Central Mali (Mopti)**: Intercommunal violence, armed group attacks on villages
- **Bamako**: Capital, relatively stable but periodic security incidents
- **Menaka/Gao**: ISGS activity, civilian targeting

**Burkina Faso - Most Affected Areas:**
- **Sahel Region (north)**: JNIM and ISGS control large areas
- **Nord Region**: Armed group presence, displacement
- **Est Region**: Increasing attacks, displacement
- **Centre-Nord**: Blockade of some towns by armed groups
- Over 40% of territory outside government control

**Niger - Most Affected Areas:**
- **Tillaberi Region**: Tri-border area (Mali-Niger-Burkina), ISGS activity
- **Diffa Region**: Boko Haram/ISWAP spillover from Nigeria
- **Tahoua Region**: Armed group activity increasing
- **Agadez Region**: Migration routes, armed banditry

## Health and Medical Access

**Mali Hospitals:**
- **Bamako**: Gabriel Toure Hospital, Point G Hospital, Mali Hospital
- **Mopti**: Mopti Regional Hospital, MSF-supported facilities
- **Gao**: Gao Regional Hospital
- **Timbuktu**: Timbuktu Regional Hospital (limited services)

**Burkina Faso Hospitals:**
- **Ouagadougou**: CHU Yalgado Ouedraogo, CHU de Bogodogo
- **Bobo-Dioulasso**: CHU Souro Sanou
- **Kaya**: Regional hospital (under strain from displacement)
- **Djibo**: Hospital under de facto blockade by armed groups

**Niger Hospitals:**
- **Niamey**: Hopital National de Niamey, Hopital de Reference de Niamey
- **Zinder**: Hopital National de Zinder
- **Maradi**: Hopital de Reference de Maradi
- **Diffa**: Diffa Regional Hospital

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Malaria: Leading cause of death in all three countries
- Meningitis: Sahel sits in the "meningitis belt"
- Cholera outbreaks during rainy season
- Severe malnutrition (chronic and acute) widespread
- Measles outbreaks in displacement settings
- Very limited healthcare in rural and conflict areas
- Armed groups have attacked health facilities

## Water and Food Security

**Water:**
- Water scarcity is fundamental challenge across the Sahel
- Dry season (October-June) particularly severe
- Wells and boreholes primary water sources in rural areas
- UNICEF and NGOs support water infrastructure
- Water points sometimes targeted or contested by armed groups

**Food:**
- Chronic food insecurity across the region
- Sahel experiences annual "lean season" (June-September)
- Armed group blockades of towns cut off food supplies (especially Burkina Faso)
- WFP and partners provide emergency food assistance
- Pastoral communities affected by drought and insecurity

## Displacement

**Scale:**
- **Mali**: 400,000+ IDPs
- **Burkina Faso**: 2+ million IDPs (one of fastest-growing displacement crises globally)
- **Niger**: 700,000+ IDPs and refugees from Nigeria, Mali

**Displacement Settings:**
- Primarily urban displacement (IDPs move to towns)
- Host communities under severe strain
- Formal camps in some locations
- Many displaced in informal settings without services

## Security

**Armed Group Threats:**
- JNIM (Al-Qaeda affiliate): Active across all three countries
- ISGS (Islamic State affiliate): Tri-border area primarily
- Boko Haram/ISWAP: Niger's Diffa region
- Attacks on villages, markets, religious sites, military targets
- IEDs on roads throughout conflict areas

**Military Juntas:**
- All three countries under military rule
- France's military withdrawal complete
- Russian Wagner/Africa Corps forces present in Mali and Burkina Faso
- State of emergency in effect in various regions

**Safety Recommendations:**
- Avoid travel in rural areas of conflict-affected regions
- Stay on main roads between major cities
- Avoid night travel
- Carry identification at all times
- Monitor local security updates
- Avoid large gatherings and markets in conflict areas

## Communication

**Phone and Internet:**
- Mobile networks functional in urban areas (Orange, Moov, Airtel, MTN depending on country)
- Mobile money (Orange Money) widely used
- Limited or no coverage in rural conflict areas
- Internet shutdowns around political events

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- CFA Franc (XOF) - shared currency across West Africa
- Pegged to Euro
- Mobile money widely used (Orange Money, Moov Money)
- Cash essential in rural areas
- Banking infrastructure limited outside capitals

Remember: The Sahel crisis is worsening and expanding. Armed groups control vast territories with limited state presence. Humanitarian access is severely constrained. Always verify current conditions and travel only with proper security assessment.
