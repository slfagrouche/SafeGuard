# Somalia Safety Information

## Current Conflict Context

Somalia faces a complex crisis involving armed conflict with Al-Shabaab, clan-based disputes, political instability, climate emergencies (drought and flooding), and widespread displacement. The federal government, with African Union (ATMIS) support, has been conducting military operations against Al-Shabaab.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **South-Central Somalia**: Al-Shabaab stronghold areas (Lower Shabelle, Middle Shabelle, Bay, Bakool)
- **Mogadishu**: Capital, periodic Al-Shabaab attacks (bombings, targeted killings)
- **Benadir Region**: Urban displacement crisis around Mogadishu
- **Galmudug**: Clan conflicts and Al-Shabaab activity
- **Hirshabelle**: Frontline areas, civilian displacement

**Relatively Safer Areas:**
- **Somaliland** (northwest, self-declared independent): Hargeisa relatively stable
- **Puntland** (northeast, semi-autonomous): Garowe, Bossaso relatively stable
- Parts of Mogadishu's fortified zones (Halane/airport compound area)

**UNHCR Somalia:**
- Mogadishu Office

- Camp management and shelter programs

**World Food Programme (WFP):**
- Emergency food assistance to millions
- School feeding programs
- Resilience and livelihood support

**AMISOM/ATMIS (African Union Mission):**
- Security operations against Al-Shabaab
- Protection of key installations and routes
- Supports humanitarian access in liberated areas

## Health and Medical Access

**Hospitals:**
- **Mogadishu**: Banadir Hospital (Medina), De Martino Hospital, SOS Hospital, Keysaney Hospital
- **Hargeisa (Somaliland)**: Hargeisa Group Hospital, Edna Adan Hospital (maternal care)
- **Garowe (Puntland)**: Garowe Regional Hospital
- **Baidoa**: Bay Regional Hospital (MSF-supported)
- **Kismayo**: Kismayo General Hospital

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Cholera outbreaks recurring, especially during rainy season
- Measles and polio outbreaks
- Acute watery diarrhea endemic
- Severe acute malnutrition - one of highest rates globally
- Malaria prevalent in southern regions
- Limited healthcare infrastructure outside major towns
- Very few qualified healthcare workers

**Disease Prevention:**
- Treat all water (boil or use purification)
- Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS) for diarrhea
- Mosquito nets for malaria prevention
- Seek medical care for any fever, severe diarrhea, or respiratory illness

## Water and Food Security

**Water:**
- Severe water scarcity due to recurring drought
- Urban water systems in poor condition
- Water trucking critical in drought-affected areas
- UNICEF and NGOs support water points in IDP settlements
- Water-related diseases among top killers

**Food:**
- WFP food distribution ongoing but funding shortfalls
- Livestock deaths from drought devastating pastoral communities
- Local markets functional but prices volatile
- Severe food insecurity affecting over 5 million people
- Famine conditions have occurred in recent years in worst-hit areas

## Displacement

**IDP Settlements:**
- Over 3.8 million IDPs across Somalia
- Massive IDP settlements around Mogadishu (Daynille, Kaxda, Kaaraan)
- Baidoa: Major IDP concentration
- Settlements often informal with minimal services
- Evictions of IDPs common in Mogadishu

**Refugee Camps:**
- Dadaab (Kenya) and Dollo Ado (Ethiopia) host large Somali refugee populations
- Returns occurring but conditions often not conducive

**Climate Displacement:**
- Drought displacing pastoralist communities from rural areas to towns
- Flooding (Shabelle and Juba rivers) causes periodic mass displacement
- El Nino/La Nina weather patterns intensify displacement

## Security

**Al-Shabaab:**
- Active across south-central Somalia
- Vehicle-borne IEDs (VBIEDs) and suicide attacks in urban areas
- Roadside bombs on major highways
- Targeted assassinations of government officials, journalists
- Avoid large gatherings, government buildings, hotels, restaurants in Mogadishu

**Clan Dynamics:**
- Clan-based conflicts over resources, land, and political power
- Understanding local clan dynamics important for safety
- Seek local guidance before traveling between clan territories

**Checkpoints:**
- Government, ATMIS, and clan militia checkpoints widespread
- Carry valid identification
- Al-Shabaab controls checkpoints on some rural roads
- Travel in groups and during daylight only

**Piracy:**
- Maritime piracy risk along Somali coast (reduced but present)
- Avoid unescorted sea travel

## Specific Risks

**Gender-Based Violence:**
- Extremely high rates, especially in IDP settings
- Female genital mutilation/cutting prevalence among highest globally
- Limited services available - seek UNFPA, UNHCR protection
- Elman Peace and Human Rights Centre provides some GBV services in Mogadishu

**Child Protection:**
- Al-Shabaab recruitment of children
- Child labor and exploitation
- UNICEF child protection programming
- Child marriage rates among highest globally

**Journalists and Media:**
- Somalia among world's most dangerous for journalists
- Media workers face targeted attacks
- Exercise extreme caution with information sharing

## Communication

**Phone and Internet:**
- Mobile networks: Hormuud (largest), Somtel, Golis (Puntland)
- Mobile money (EVC Plus via Hormuud) widely used - primary payment method
- Internet available in major cities via mobile data
- Solar charging widespread due to no national power grid

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- Somali Shilling official but US dollars widely preferred
- Mobile money (EVC Plus) is dominant payment method
- No functional banking system for most of population
- Hawala system (Dahabshiil, Amal) for transfers
- Cash (USD) essential for travel outside major cities

Remember: Somalia's security situation is extremely fluid. Al-Shabaab maintains shadow governance in many areas. Always seek current security information from local contacts and humanitarian organizations. Do not travel between cities without security assessment and arrangement.
