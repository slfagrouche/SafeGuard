# Yemen Safety Information

## Current Conflict Context

Yemen has been in conflict since 2014, primarily between the internationally recognized government (backed by Saudi-led coalition) and Ansar Allah (Houthi) forces. The conflict has created one of the world's worst humanitarian crises, with over 21 million people in need of humanitarian assistance.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **Hodeidah**: Strategic port city, critical for humanitarian aid imports, frequent fighting
- **Marib**: Frontline city, heavy fighting and displacement, oil-rich area contested
- **Taiz**: Besieged city with severe access restrictions, divided between warring parties
- **Sa'ada**: Northern stronghold, frequent airstrikes and cross-border operations
- **Aden**: Southern port, intermittent armed clashes, governance instability
- **Hajjah and Al Hudaydah governorates**: Severe food insecurity and disease outbreaks

**Relatively Safer Areas (as of latest reports):**
- Parts of Hadramawt governorate (eastern Yemen)
- Some areas of Socotra Island
- Parts of Al Mahrah governorate (far east)

## Health and Medical Access

**Functioning Hospitals (status highly variable):**
- **Sana'a**: Al-Thawra Hospital, Al-Kuwait Hospital, Al-Jomhouri Hospital
- **Aden**: Al-Sadaqa Hospital, Aden General Hospital, 22 May Hospital
- **Taiz**: Al-Thawra Hospital (limited access due to siege), Al-Rawdha Hospital
- **Hodeidah**: Al-Thawra Hospital Hodeidah
- **Marib**: Marib General Hospital (under frequent strain from frontline casualties)

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Cholera outbreaks: Over 2.5 million suspected cases since 2016
- Diphtheria, measles, and dengue outbreaks recurring
- Severe shortage of medications and medical supplies
- Many hospitals non-functional (destroyed or lacking staff/supplies)
- Maternal and child mortality rates among highest globally

**Disease Prevention:**
- Treat all drinking water (boil 1 minute or use purification tablets)
- Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS) critical for cholera/diarrhea
- Seek medical care immediately for watery diarrhea, high fever
- Mosquito protection for dengue and malaria prevention

## Water and Food Security

**Water Access:**
- Municipal water systems severely damaged across the country
- UNICEF and NGOs support water trucking in critical areas
- Seek water distribution points organized by WFP, UNICEF, local NGOs
- Always treat water before drinking in conflict areas

**Food Distribution:**
- WFP distributes food to 13+ million people
- Distribution schedules announced through local community leaders
- Bring registration documentation and family identification
- Severe malnutrition crisis especially affecting children under 5
- MUAC screening available at nutrition centers and health facilities

**Markets:**
- Functioning but with extremely high prices due to currency collapse
- Fuel shortages affect transportation and food prices
- Cash may be scarce - some areas use mobile money (MTN, Y-Mobile)

## Safe Corridors and Displacement

**Major IDP Concentrations:**
- **Marib**: Over 1 million IDPs (largest concentration)
- **Aden**: Large IDP populations from northern governorates
- **Lahj**: Displacement camp communities
- **Hajjah**: Multiple displacement sites near Ethiopian border
- **Dhamar and Ibb**: IDPs from Taiz and Hodeidah

**Displacement Routes:**
- Northern Yemen to Marib (primary route for Houthi-controlled areas)
- Taiz to Aden corridor (dangerous, front-line crossing)
- To Djibouti and Horn of Africa (maritime route, dangerous)
- To Oman (eastern border, limited access)

## Security and Movement

**Checkpoints:**
- Multiple armed groups operate checkpoints across Yemen
- Carry identification documents at all times
- Do not photograph military installations or checkpoints
- Avoid travel at night
- Front-line areas extremely dangerous - coordinate with humanitarian actors

**Landmines and UXO:**
- Yemen heavily contaminated with landmines and unexploded ordnance
- Particularly hazardous: Hodeidah, Marib, Taiz, Sa'ada areas
- Stay on paved roads and well-traveled paths
- Never touch unknown objects
- Report UXO to UNDP/YEMAC demining teams

**Communication:**
- Internet and cellular service disrupted in many areas
- Two separate telecommunications networks (Houthi-controlled and government-controlled)
- Power outages common - solar charging panels increasingly used
- Keep emergency contacts saved offline

## Specific Risks

**Gender-Based Violence:**
- Widespread in displacement settings
- Early and forced marriage rates have increased
- Seek protection services from UNHCR, UNFPA
- Women's safe spaces available in some IDP camps

**Child Protection:**
- Risk of recruitment by armed groups
- Child labor and exploitation increasing
- Severe malnutrition affecting millions of children
- UNICEF child protection services in accessible areas

**Cholera and Disease:**
- Wash hands frequently with soap and water
- Drink only treated/boiled water
- Seek immediate medical care for severe diarrhea
- ORS packets available from health facilities and pharmacies

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- Yemeni Rial severely devalued, two exchange rates (Houthi vs government areas)
- Saudi Riyals and US dollars accepted in many transactions
- Mobile money services (MTN Yemen) available in some areas
- ATMs largely non-functional

Remember: Yemen's situation is among the world's most severe humanitarian crises. Information changes rapidly. Always seek current guidance from humanitarian organizations on the ground.
