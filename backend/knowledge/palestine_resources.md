# Palestine Resources and Safety Information

## Humanitarian Context

The Palestinian territories, particularly Gaza, face ongoing humanitarian crisis conditions due to prolonged conflict, blockade, and periodic military operations. The West Bank experiences movement restrictions, settlement expansion, and periodic escalations. Access to basic services, medical care, and humanitarian assistance varies significantly by location and current security situation.

**Gaza Strip:**
- Under blockade since 2007, severely limiting movement of goods and people
- Periodic escalations result in infrastructure damage and civilian casualties
- Healthcare system under severe strain with limited supplies
- Population density among highest in world, increasing vulnerability

**West Bank:**
- Movement restricted by checkpoints, permit system, and separation barrier
- Areas classified as A (Palestinian Authority control), B (joint), C (Israeli control)
- Access to services varies significantly by area classification
- East Jerusalem faces particular restrictions and administrative challenges

## Key Organizations

**United Nations Relief and Works Agency (UNRWA):**
- Primary UN agency for Palestine refugees
- Operates schools, health clinics, food assistance programs
- Gaza Hotline: +970-8-677-7527
- West Bank: +970-2-290-8280
- Provides emergency assistance during escalations
- Maintains health centers and distribution points across both territories

**Palestine Red Crescent Society (PRCS):**
- Emergency medical services and ambulance response
- Emergency Number: +970-2-240-6515
- Gaza: +970-8-286-7555
- West Bank: +970-2-240-6515
- Operates hospitals, clinics, and emergency response teams
- Primary responder for medical emergencies and evacuations

**World Health Organization (WHO) Palestine:**
- Coordinates health sector response during emergencies
- Supports Palestinian Ministry of Health
- Emergency health supplies and coordination
- Contact through UNRWA or local health facilities

**International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC):**
- Protection services for civilians
- Family reunification and tracing
- Detention visits and protection monitoring
- Gaza: +972-8-282-0381

**Medical Aid for Palestinians (MAP):**
- Long-term healthcare support
- Mobile clinics in underserved areas
- Mental health and psychosocial support

## Water Access Points

**Gaza:**
- Municipal water severely contaminated (95%+ not meeting WHO standards)
- Seawater desalination plants (when operational)
- UNRWA distribution points during emergencies
- Water trucking services (private and humanitarian)
- Groundwater extremely saline - not safe for drinking without treatment

**West Bank:**
- Water access controlled by Israeli authorities
- Palestinian communities face water restrictions
- Tanker water delivery in some areas (expensive)
- UNRWA and local NGOs provide emergency water during shortages

## Shelter and Aid Access

**UNRWA Emergency Shelters (Gaza):**
- Schools converted to emergency shelters during escalations
- Coordinates with Palestinian authorities
- Registration required for assistance
- Overcrowding typical during active conflict

**West Bank Protection Concerns:**
- Communities at risk of displacement (Area C)
- Legal support organizations: HaMoked, B'Tselem, Al-Haq
- UN OCHA monitors and reports on displacement risks

**Food Assistance:**
- UNRWA food distribution to registered refugees
- Local charities and Islamic organizations provide assistance
- Gaza: Chronic food insecurity affects 68%+ of population
- West Bank: Growing food insecurity in Area C and refugee camps

## Movement and Access

**Gaza Crossings:**
- **Rafah Crossing (Egypt):** Primarily for individuals, frequently closed
- **Erez Crossing (Israel):** Severely restricted, permits required and rarely granted
- **Kerem Shalom (Commercial):** Goods only, controlled by Israel
- Crossings often close during security escalations

**West Bank Checkpoints:**
- Over 700 permanent and temporary obstacles to movement
- Qalandia checkpoint (Ramallah-Jerusalem) frequently congested
- Permit system required for many movements, especially to Jerusalem
- Checkpoint waits can be hours, unpredictable closures common

**Travel Guidance:**
- Carry ID at all times
- Checkpoints: Follow instructions, do not argue, do not photograph
- Plan extra time for any journey due to unpredictable delays
- Some roads restricted to Israeli vehicles only (West Bank)

## Emergency Procedures

**During Escalation:**
- Stay informed through local radio, UNRWA alerts, trusted news sources
- If bombing/shelling: Seek interior room away from windows, lowest floor
- Monitor PRCS ambulance routes during active conflict
- Stock emergency supplies: water, food, medications, flashlight, first aid
- Charge phones and power banks when electricity available

**Evacuation Orders:**
- Israel sometimes issues evacuation warnings before strikes (leaflets, phone calls, "roof knocking")
- Evacuation time often minimal (minutes to hours)
- UNRWA schools traditionally used as shelters in Gaza
- Coordinate with PRCS or local authorities when possible

**Medical Emergency:**
- Call Palestine Red Crescent emergency number
- Be prepared to describe exact location clearly
- Ambulance access may be delayed at checkpoints
- In urgent situations, private transport may be faster but risky during active conflict

## Specific Vulnerabilities

**Healthcare Access:**
- Severe shortage of medications, especially in Gaza
- Medical equipment often broken due to lack of spare parts
- Chronic disease medications (insulin, cancer treatments) face severe shortages
- Exit permits for medical treatment outside Gaza rarely granted, often delayed

**Electricity:**
- Gaza: Severe electricity shortages (4-8 hours per day typically)
- West Bank: Generally stable but periodic outages
- Affects water pumping, hospitals, refrigeration of medicines

**Mental Health:**
- High rates of trauma, especially among children
- UNRWA, PRCS, and local NGOs provide psychosocial support services
- Community-based support groups in many areas

**Protection from Violence:**
- Civilians face risks from military operations, airstrikes, armed clashes
- Women's protection services available through local NGOs
- Child protection concerns, especially during escalations
- Document incidents when safe to do so

Remember: Situation in Palestinian territories changes rapidly during escalations. This information provides general guidance but always seek current information from UNRWA, PRCS, and other humanitarian actors on the ground. Verify current status of services before relying on them.
