# Central African Republic (CAR) Safety Information

## Current Conflict Context

The Central African Republic has experienced chronic armed conflict since 2012, involving multiple armed groups (anti-balaka militias, ex-Seleka factions), government forces supported by Russian Wagner/Africa Corps, and UN peacekeepers (MINUSCA). Despite peace agreements, violence continues across much of the country.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **Bangassou and Haut-Mbomou**: Armed group clashes, displacement
- **Bambari and Ouaka**: Multiple armed groups, intercommunal violence
- **Bria and Haute-Kotto**: UPC and other armed group activity
- **Northwest (Paoua, Bocaranga)**: 3R and other armed group operations
- **Kaga-Bandoro**: Armed group presence, displacement

**Relatively Safer Areas:**
- Central Bangui (capital, relative security)
- Some areas near MINUSCA bases
- Parts of southern CAR along Congo border

## Health and Medical Access

**Hospitals:**
- **Bangui**: Complexe Pediatrique de Bangui, Hopital Communautaire, Hopital de l'Amitie, MSF surgical program
- **Bambari**: Regional hospital, MSF-supported
- **Bria**: Regional hospital (limited)
- **Kaga-Bandoro**: Hospital, MSF-supported
- **Paoua**: MSF hospital

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Malaria: Leading cause of death (hyperendemic)
- HIV/AIDS: High prevalence
- Measles outbreaks in displacement settings
- Acute malnutrition widespread
- Very few healthcare workers (1 doctor per 50,000+ people)
- Health facilities attacked and looted during fighting
- Mpox cases reported

## Water and Food Security

**Water:**
- Safe water access very limited outside Bangui
- Rivers and wells primary sources
- Treat all water before drinking
- UNICEF supports water infrastructure

**Food:**
- Over half the population food insecure
- WFP food distribution in accessible areas
- Agricultural production disrupted by conflict
- Markets functional but prices high

## Displacement

**Internal Displacement:**
- Over 500,000 IDPs within CAR
- Multiple IDP sites around Bangui and in provinces
- Refugees in neighboring countries (Cameroon, Chad, DRC)
- Displacement driven by armed group violence and intercommunal clashes

## Security

**Armed Groups:**
- Multiple factions of ex-Seleka (UPC, FPRC, MPC)
- Anti-balaka militias
- 3R (Return, Reclamation, Rehabilitation) in northwest
- CPC (Coalition of Patriots for Change) coalition
- Russian Wagner/Africa Corps supporting government

**Safety:**
- Carry identification at all times
- Avoid travel outside Bangui without security arrangement
- Road travel extremely dangerous due to armed groups and bandits
- MINUSCA may provide security for humanitarian movements
- Night travel prohibited in most areas

**Landmines/UXO:**
- Some contamination from previous conflicts
- ERW (explosive remnants of war) in former conflict areas

## Communication

**Phone and Internet:**
- Mobile networks: Orange, Moov (Telecel)
- Coverage limited outside Bangui and major towns
- Mobile money (Orange Money) available
- Internet limited and expensive

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- Central African CFA Franc (XAF)
- Cash economy dominant
- Very limited banking infrastructure
- Mobile money growing
- US dollars and euros accepted for some transactions

Remember: CAR is one of the world's most dangerous countries for humanitarian workers. Armed group control shifts frequently. Travel outside Bangui requires careful security planning and often MINUSCA coordination. Verify current conditions before any movement.
