# Ethiopia Safety Information

## Current Conflict Context

Ethiopia faces multiple concurrent crises: the aftermath of the Tigray conflict (cessation of hostilities agreement signed November 2022), ongoing conflicts in Amhara and Oromia regions, and intercommunal violence in several areas. The humanitarian situation remains severe with millions displaced.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **Tigray**: Post-conflict recovery, severe infrastructure destruction, intermittent access issues
- **Amhara Region**: Active conflict between federal forces and Fano militia, state of emergency declared
- **Oromia Region**: Ongoing operations against OLA (Oromo Liberation Army), particularly in western and southern Oromia
- **Afar Region**: Conflict spillover and intercommunal violence
- **Benishangul-Gumuz**: Ethnic violence and displacement
- **Somali Region**: Drought-related humanitarian crisis

**Relatively Safer Areas:**
- Addis Ababa (capital, generally stable)
- Parts of SNNPR (Southern Nations)
- Dire Dawa and Harar (eastern cities)
- Some areas of Sidama region

## Health and Medical Access

**Hospitals:**
- **Addis Ababa**: Tikur Anbessa (Black Lion) Hospital, St. Paul's Hospital, Zewditu Memorial Hospital, ALERT Hospital
- **Mekelle (Tigray)**: Ayder Comprehensive Specialized Hospital, Mekelle General Hospital (rehabilitation phase)
- **Bahir Dar (Amhara)**: Felege Hiwot Comprehensive Hospital
- **Hawassa (Sidama)**: Hawassa University Hospital
- **Dire Dawa**: Dil Chora Hospital
- **Jimma**: Jimma University Medical Center

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Malaria: Major health threat, especially below 2,000m altitude
- Cholera and acute watery diarrhea outbreaks
- Measles outbreaks in conflict-affected areas
- Severe malnutrition in Tigray, Amhara, Somali regions
- Healthcare infrastructure destroyed in conflict areas
- Medication shortages in Tigray and Amhara

**Healthcare Access in Conflict Areas:**
- Tigray: Health system slowly recovering, many facilities destroyed or damaged
- Amhara: Healthcare disrupted in areas of active fighting
- Oromia: Variable access depending on location
- Many health facilities in conflict areas non-functional

## Water and Food Security

**Water:**
- Urban water systems generally functional in major cities
- Rural areas dependent on wells and springs
- Drought severely affecting Somali, Oromia, SNNPR pastoral areas
- Water infrastructure damaged in Tigray conflict
- UNICEF and NGOs support water trucking in drought areas

**Food:**
- WFP food distribution in crisis areas
- Aid access restricted or intermittent in some conflict areas
- Markets functional in urban areas but prices high
- Agricultural production disrupted by conflict and drought
- Over 20 million people food insecure

## Displacement

**IDP Concentrations:**
- **Tigray**: Over 1 million IDPs within the region
- **Amhara**: Hundreds of thousands displaced by ongoing conflict
- **Oromia**: Significant displacement in western areas
- **Afar**: IDPs from Tigray conflict and local tensions
- **Somali Region**: Drought-displaced pastoral communities

**Refugee Camps:**
- Gambella: South Sudanese refugees
- Afar/Tigray: Eritrean refugees
- Somali Region: Somali refugees
- UNHCR manages multiple camp settings

## Security

**Amhara Conflict:**
- Federal military operations against Fano militia
- Internet shutdowns common in affected areas
- Curfews and movement restrictions
- State of emergency provisions in effect

**Oromia Operations:**
- Government operations against OLA
- Sporadic attacks on civilians and infrastructure
- Road safety concerns in western and southern Oromia

**General Security:**
- Checkpoints operated by military and security forces
- Carry national ID (kebele ID) or passport at all times
- Internet and telecommunications shutdowns in conflict areas common
- Avoid large demonstrations and political gatherings
- Photography of military installations prohibited

**Intercommunal Violence:**
- Ethnic tensions in multiple regions
- Avoid areas with reported ethnic clashes
- Be aware of local dynamics when traveling between regions

## Communication

**Phone and Internet:**
- Ethio Telecom (sole operator, state-owned)
- Safaricom Ethiopia entering market
- Internet shutdowns frequent in conflict areas (weeks to months)
- Voice calls may be monitored
- Limited mobile money (Telebirr gaining adoption)

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- Ethiopian Birr (ETB)
- US dollars useful for hotels and some transactions
- Commercial Bank of Ethiopia and Awash Bank have ATMs in major cities
- Mobile money (Telebirr) growing rapidly
- Cash essential outside major cities
- Informal economy significant

Remember: Ethiopia's humanitarian situation is complex with multiple simultaneous crises. Communications blackouts in conflict areas mean information may be delayed. Always verify current security conditions through humanitarian organizations and local contacts before travel.
