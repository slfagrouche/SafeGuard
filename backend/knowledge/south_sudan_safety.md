# South Sudan Safety Information

## Current Conflict Context

South Sudan, the world's youngest nation (independent since 2011), has been mired in civil conflict since 2013. Despite the 2018 Revitalized Peace Agreement, subnational violence continues across multiple states. Over 2 million refugees have fled and 2+ million remain internally displaced.

**Most Affected Areas:**
- **Upper Nile**: Armed clashes between rival factions, Malakal area
- **Jonglei**: Intercommunal violence, cattle raids, armed group activity
- **Unity State**: Flooding and armed conflict combined
- **Western Equatoria**: National Salvation Front (NAS) activity
- **Central Equatoria**: Armed group operations along Juba-Nimule road
- **Lakes State**: Intercommunal violence over cattle and resources

**Relatively Safer Areas:**
- Parts of Juba city center
- Some areas of Western Bahr el Ghazal
- Northern Bahr el Ghazal (Aweil area, relatively)

## Health and Medical Access

**Hospitals:**
- **Juba**: Juba Teaching Hospital, Al Sabah Children's Hospital, Star of the Sea Health Centre
- **Malakal**: MSF hospital, Malakal Teaching Hospital
- **Bentiu**: MSF hospital
- **Wau**: Wau Teaching Hospital
- **Bor**: Bor Hospital (limited services)

**Critical Health Concerns:**
- Malaria: Leading cause of death, endemic across the country
- Kala-azar (visceral leishmaniasis) in some areas
- Cholera outbreaks recurring, especially during rainy season
- Measles and meningitis outbreaks
- Severe malnutrition - among worst globally
- Snakebite - significant cause of morbidity and mortality
- Very few healthcare workers (one of lowest doctor-per-population ratios globally)

**Disease Prevention:**
- Sleep under insecticide-treated mosquito nets (malaria prevention)
- Treat all water before drinking
- ORS for diarrheal diseases
- Avoid walking through tall grass (snakebite prevention)
- Seek medical care early for any fever

## Water and Food Security

**Water:**
- Safe water access among lowest in the world
- Many areas dependent on boreholes and rivers
- Annual flooding contaminates water sources
- UNICEF and NGOs drill boreholes and support water trucking
- Always treat water (boil, filter, or use purification tablets)

**Food:**
- WFP feeds millions annually but access constrained
- Severe food insecurity affecting 7.7+ million people
- Agricultural production disrupted by flooding and conflict
- Fishing and pastoral communities affected by environmental changes
- Famine conditions in some areas during lean seasons

## Displacement

**Protection of Civilians Sites:**
- Bentiu PoC: Largest with 100,000+ displaced
- Malakal PoC: Significant population
- Managed by UNMISS with humanitarian partners
- Provide relative security but overcrowded

**Flooding:**
- Catastrophic annual flooding displaces hundreds of thousands
- Unity, Jonglei, Upper Nile states worst affected
- Flood waters can persist for months
- Destroys crops, shelters, and infrastructure

## Security

**Subnational Violence:**
- Cattle raids with increasing use of automatic weapons
- Intercommunal revenge attacks
- Road ambushes common, especially during dry season
- Recruitment of youth into armed groups

**Movement:**
- Roads largely unpaved and impassable during rainy season
- Road travel extremely dangerous due to ambushes and armed groups
- UNHAS (UN Humanitarian Air Service) provides air transport between major towns
- Armed escorts sometimes necessary for road movement
- Travel only during daylight

**Checkpoints:**
- Government, SPLA-IO, and local armed group checkpoints
- Carry valid identification
- Remain calm, cooperative
- Avoid carrying large amounts of cash visibly

**Wildlife:**
- Hippos and crocodiles in rivers (significant risk during water collection)
- Snakes prevalent - wear boots, use flashlight at night
- Seek anti-venom treatment immediately for snakebites

## Specific Risks

**Gender-Based Violence:**
- Conflict-related sexual violence widespread
- UNFPA and IRC provide some GBV services
- PoC sites have women's protection programs
- Limited access to post-rape care in most areas

**Child Soldiers:**
- Both government and opposition forces recruited children
- UNICEF supports release and reintegration programs
- Report child recruitment to UNMISS Child Protection

**Intercommunal Violence:**
- Cattle-related conflicts escalate with availability of weapons
- Ethnic dimensions to many conflicts
- Community protection mechanisms important
- Seek UNMISS protection if targeted

## Communication

**Phone and Internet:**
- Mobile networks: Zain (primary), MTN
- Coverage limited to major towns and along some roads
- Mobile money (m-Gurush, MTN MoMo) growing
- No internet in most rural areas
- Solar charging essential (no national power grid)

## Financial Considerations

**Currency:**
- South Sudanese Pound (SSP) severely devalued
- US dollars essential for most transactions
- Mobile money services expanding
- No functional banking outside major towns
- Cash economy dominant - keep small denominations

Remember: South Sudan is one of the world's most challenging humanitarian environments. Infrastructure is minimal, healthcare nearly non-existent outside major towns, and violence is unpredictable. Movement by road is extremely risky. Always coordinate with humanitarian organizations and seek security briefs before any travel.
